## 🧠 From Pattern Recognition to Reasoning: ARC Prize 2026 — Paper Track

### 📌 Project Description

This project investigates one of the most critical limitations of modern artificial intelligence: its heavy reliance on **pattern recognition** without genuine understanding or reasoning. While current AI systems achieve impressive performance across various domains, they often fail when required to solve problems that demand **abstraction, logical inference, and generalization beyond training data**.

In the context of the Abstraction and Reasoning Corpus (ARC), this limitation becomes especially evident. ARC is specifically designed to evaluate an AI system’s ability to **learn new concepts from minimal examples** and apply them to unseen tasks—something that humans do naturally but machines still struggle with.

This research proposes a transition **from pattern recognition to reasoning** by developing a hybrid framework that combines **data-driven learning** with **rule-based and symbolic reasoning mechanisms**. Instead of merely mapping inputs to outputs, the system is designed to identify underlying structures, infer transformation rules, and adapt its strategy dynamically across different problem settings.

The core contribution of this project lies in enabling the AI system to:

* Move beyond surface-level pattern matching
* Extract **abstract representations** from limited examples
* Perform **logical reasoning** over grid-based transformations
* Generalize learned concepts to entirely new tasks

To achieve this, the proposed approach emphasizes **compositional generalization**, where complex problems are decomposed into smaller, interpretable components. These components can then be recombined in flexible ways, allowing the system to handle a wide variety of tasks without requiring extensive retraining.

Furthermore, the project explores how integrating **adaptive learning mechanisms** with structured reasoning processes can significantly improve performance on ARC benchmarks. This hybrid strategy reflects a more human-like approach to intelligence—where learning, reasoning, and adaptation work together rather than in isolation.

---

### 🎯 Research Objectives

* Transition AI systems from pattern recognition toward **true reasoning capabilities**
* Improve performance on ARC tasks through **generalization and abstraction**
* Develop a hybrid model combining **neural and symbolic approaches**
* Enable learning from **few examples (few-shot learning)**
* Investigate methods for **adaptive and compositional problem solving**

---

### ⚙️ Methodological Focus

* Pattern extraction from grid-based inputs
* Rule inference and symbolic transformation
* Adaptive reasoning pipelines
* Hybrid architectures (learning + logic)
* Evaluation on unseen ARC tasks

---

### 🚀 Vision & Impact

This project contributes to the broader pursuit of **Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)** by addressing a fundamental gap in current AI systems: the lack of true reasoning ability. By shifting the focus from data dependency to **understanding and adaptability**, this research aims to build AI systems that can think more like humans—capable of solving new problems, not just recognizing familiar ones.

Ultimately, this work is a step toward creating intelligent systems that are not only accurate, but also **flexible, interpretable, and capable of reasoning in complex, unseen environments**.
